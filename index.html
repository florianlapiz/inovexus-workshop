Outbound Fundamentals
Why most outbound fails — and how to fix it
20 min

Slide 1 — The #1 Reason Outbound Fails

It's not the tools, not deliverability, not the subject line. It's that most founders pitch features instead of addressing real pain. We reframe outbound as problem-solving, not selling.

Slide 2 — The 4 Layers of Customer Pain

  • Functional: What's broken, slow, or manual?
  • Emotional: What frustrates them or keeps them up at night?
  • Social: How do they look to their boss, board, or peers?
  • Financial: Where are they losing money or missing revenue?

Key point: Emails that hit emotional + financial layers get replies. Emails that only describe features get ignored.

Slide 3 — Relevance vs. Personalization

Side-by-side comparison: a "personalized" email (generic, name-drop) vs. a "relevant" email (references what's actually happening inside the company). The audience votes which one they'd reply to.

Slide 4 — The Seed-Stage Outbound Stack

Clay (research) → Instantly (sending) → AI (copy). Three tools, ~$250/month. What each one does, how they connect. Burner domains, warm-up, verification — the basics you can't skip.

Slide 5 — Micro-Campaigns: 50 vs. 5,000

Why 50 deeply researched accounts outperform 5,000 generic contacts. Real reply rate comparison: 8-15% vs. 1-2%. For seed-stage, you need 5-10 conversations — not 75 unsubscribe replies.

2
Use Cases & Playbooks
Real examples from real campaigns
15 min

Slide 6 — Use Case: Job Postings as Intent Signals

Screenshot walkthrough: How to pull hiring data from a target company in Clay, what it reveals about their priorities, and how to turn it into an opening line that proves you did your homework.

Example: "You have 3 open SDR roles — your outbound needs to scale, but new hires take 3-6 months to ramp..."

Slide 7 — Use Case: AI Account Research at Scale

Screenshot walkthrough: How to let an AI agent in Clay analyze a company's website, positioning, and team size — then auto-generate a pain hypothesis you can drop into an email. No more 30 minutes of manual research per prospect.

Slide 8 — Use Case: The Pain-First Email

Before/after comparison of a real campaign email. The 4-sentence framework:

  • Sentence 1: Name the problem (data from Clay)
  • Sentence 2: Show the implication (why it matters now)
  • Sentence 3: Hint at the outcome (no feature list)
  • Sentence 4: Soft CTA (low commitment ask)

Under 80 words. No attachments. No HTML. Subject line: short, lowercase, specific.

Slide 9 — Use Case: Creative Data Sources

Quick overview of data sources most competitors don't use: tech stack signals, funding data, G2 reviews, website scraping. Each one with a concrete outbound angle the audience can steal.

3
Q&A — Your Questions
Pre-submitted questions + open discussion
20 min

Pre-Submitted Questions (10 min)

Alberto collects questions from the 7 startups beforehand. These are addressed first — anchored in real challenges, not hypotheticals. Typical topics:

  • How do I define my ICP when I have less than 10 customers?
  • What reply rates should I expect? When do I pivot?
  • How do I handle GDPR/compliance in cold outreach?
  • Clay vs. Apollo vs. manual research — when to use what?

Open Discussion (10 min)

Live questions from the room. Anything related to outbound, ICP, tooling, copywriting, or deliverability. The Inovexus colleague with Clay experience joins for tool-specific questions.

4
Wrap-Up & Next Steps
3 takeaways + follow-up offer
5 min

Slide 10 — Three Rules to Walk Away With

  • Problem first, tool second. If you can't describe your customer's pain in their words, no tool will save your outbound.
  • Relevance > personalization. Write emails that could only be sent to that one company.
  • 50 right > 5,000 wrong. One focused micro-campaign beats ten generic blasts.
