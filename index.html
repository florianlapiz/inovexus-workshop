Two Insights to Hold

Multi-Threading: Don't wait for the Champion to sell internally. Reach 2-3 personas in parallel, with different messages tailored to each, referencing each other.

Relevance per Persona: Same account. Same problem. Five different framings. If your email could be sent to 500 companies — or to 5 different people in the same company — it's not relevant enough.

Signal-rule: If you can't fill 3+ personas with specific pain per account, you don't know the account well enough to pitch it yet.

4
The 3 Expansion Vectors
Strategic choice — where to grow
4 min

You have three directions to expand. Most founders only see one.

Upmarket isn't the only move. And often, it's not even the smart one.

The 3 Vectors

VectorDefinitionExample (HR-Tech)
SizeSame buyer profile, bigger companies50-seat → 500-seat → 5,000-seat
VerticalSame size, different industryHR-Tech for Startups → HR-Tech for Manufacturing
Use-caseSame buyer, additional painOnboarding → Onboarding + Performance Reviews

When Which Vector?

  • Go Size (upmarket): 10+ paying customers at current size, product handles enterprise requirements (SSO, audit logs, SLAs), economics break without bigger deals. Warning: 3-6x longer cycle, 2-3x CAC.
  • Go Vertical: Core use-case transferable, 1-2 proof-points in another vertical, new vertical has budget + urgency. Warning: lose reference stories.
  • Go Use-case: Existing accounts have related pain you're not solving, expansion revenue > new logo revenue. Warning: scope creep.

The Non-Obvious Move

Don't pick one. Test two in parallel. Run 50 accounts in each of two vectors. After 60 days, kill the weaker one.

7
Stack Compression
The tool landscape is being eaten by AI primitives
3 min

The question isn't "which tool." It's "which part of your stack is being eaten by AI primitives right now."

Shift #1: Claude Code is eating enrichment and data flow

The traditional Clay workflow: visual table, no-code columns, paid enrichment per row. The new workflow: Claude Code writes scraping, enrichment, and scoring scripts. In hours, not weeks. You own the pipeline.

  • Clay is faster for 50 accounts
  • Claude Code wins at 500+ accounts and repeat use
  • The AI-native move: describe what you want to Claude — Claude writes the code

Shift #2: Exa.ai is eating intent signal discovery

Google and LinkedIn are keyword-search engines. Exa.ai is semantic/LLM-native search. You describe what you want in natural language, it matches on meaning.

Example query: "Find companies that recently raised Series A, are building outbound for the first time, and whose CEO talks publicly about pipeline challenges."

You're no longer searching for data-field matches. You're searching for states.

What this means for your stack decision

  • <100 target accounts: Clay or Apollo. Custom pipelines not worth setup cost yet.
  • 500+ accounts across vectors: Claude Code + Exa.ai economics flip.
  • 1,000+ accounts/month: Custom pipelines dominate.

Build your stack composable, not monolithic.

P2
Play 2: The Champion Hypothesis Play
Sales-Tech Startup → Mid-Market Fintech
4 min

The Champion isn't the highest title. It's the person with the highest pain-win ratio.

Sending Startup: Sales-Tech SaaS, AI Call Coaching + Recording, 20 Customers. Target: Fintech Company, 500-2000 Employees.

The Problem

In one account there's a VP Sales, Sales Enablement Manager, Director of SDR, RevOps Lead. Most founders pitch the VP Sales (highest title). Reply rate: 1-2%. The VP is pitch-fatigued.

The better move: identify who has the highest pain-win ratio.

Signal Stack for Champion Identification

SignalQuestionData Source
Public PainWho posts publicly about this pain?LinkedIn content last 90 days
Role StakeWhose KPIs are directly tied to solving this?Role description, team structure
Buying Track RecordWho has historically championed tool purchases?G2 reviews, "I use X"-posts

The Workflow (in Clay, with AI-research)

Clay runs an AI-research prompt per stakeholder:

"Analyze this person's LinkedIn profile, recent posts, role, and public activity. Score 0-10: (a) Has this person publicly discussed pain around [topic]? (b) Would solving this pain directly impact their KPIs? (c) Do they have a history of championing tool purchases?"

Example Result

Account: BrightFintech Corp, 800 Employees, 3 new SDR hires in Q2.

  • Traditional guess: VP Sales Tom Rodriguez (highest title). Champion Score: 3/10.
  • Actual Champion: Sarah Chen, Sales Enablement Manager. Champion Score: 9/10.
  • Why Sarah: 4 LinkedIn posts in 30 days about ramp time, direct Q2 OKR, track record of buying Gong + Chorus at previous company.

The Cold Email (to Sarah)

Pain-based, research-driven

Subject: your post on ramp time got me thinking

Hi Sarah,

Saw your LinkedIn post last Wednesday about new-rep ramp being your Q2 priority — especially the part about the gap between week-4 and week-8.

We helped [similar fintech] cut that gap from 4 weeks to 2 using AI call coaching. Their Enablement Manager wrote a playbook I can share.

Not pitching — more a breakdown of what their first 60 days looked like. 20 minutes this week or next?

Key Takeaway: Title-based targeting works at SMB. Mid-Market needs pain-based targeting, and that's what Champion Score finds.

The 5 Cold Emails (same account, coordinated over 2-3 weeks)

To Champion (Alex, Eng Manager)

Subject: your team's on-call rotation in 2026

To Economic Buyer (Maria, VP Eng)

To Technical Buyer (Raj, Head of SRE)

Hey [Name], running a short survey with senior engineers on on-call during peak seasons. No pitch — I'll share aggregated data back. 10 minutes?

To Blocker (Sam, Head of InfoSec)

Subject: SOC2 + ISO27001 docs, just in case
